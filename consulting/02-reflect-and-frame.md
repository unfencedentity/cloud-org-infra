# Reflect and Frame

## Purpose

After discovery, the goal is to reflect back what was heard in a way that creates clarity and alignment.

This step is not a solution proposal.  
It is a concise problem definition that makes the risk visible.

---

## Output

A single sentence that captures the real issue behind the symptoms.

It should be:

- neutral in tone
- focused on predictability and risk
- easy for both technical and non-technical stakeholders to repeat

---

## Framing Template

Use this structure:

**"Right now, the main risk is that <current foundation is not predictable/reproducible>, which makes <future change/growth> harder and riskier than it needs to be."**

Examples:

- "Right now, the main risk is that the foundation evolved organically without a consistent operating model, which makes change and scaling riskier than it needs to be."
- "Right now, the main risk is that the environment works day to day but isn’t reliably reproducible, which increases risk as more teams depend on it."
- "Right now, the main risk is inconsistent standards across environments, which makes governance and safe automation difficult as the company grows."

---

## What This Step Avoids

- blaming the team or past decisions
- listing technical issues
- naming tools or implementation details
- jumping into architecture recommendations

---

## Why It Works

- validates the current reality without criticism
- creates contrast between "works today" and "safe to grow"
- aligns stakeholders around the same definition of the problem
- naturally sets up the next step (assessment) without selling

---

## Notes

If the framing sentence feels long, remove detail, not clarity.  
The objective is a sentence that can be repeated verbatim by the client.
