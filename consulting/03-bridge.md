# Bridge

## Purpose

The bridge step connects the framed problem to the engagement direction without pitching a solution.

Its role is to explain *how* the problem is typically addressed, not *what* will be implemented.

---

## Core Principle

Foundational instability should be addressed before optimization, tooling, or platform expansion.

The focus is on stabilizing the baseline first.

---

## Bridge Statement

Use a short, neutral transition such as:

- "What I usually help teams with at this stage is stabilizing the foundation before building further on top of it."
- "In situations like this, the first step is typically to bring clarity and consistency to the foundation."
- "Before introducing more automation or tools, it helps to make the foundation predictable and safe to change."

---

## What This Step Does

- connects the problem to a logical direction
- keeps the conversation outcome-focused
- avoids tools and implementation details
- prepares the ground for the next step

---

## What This Step Avoids

- selling a package
- explaining how the work is done
- naming technologies or platforms
- committing to scope or timelines

---

## Notes

If this step feels like a pitch, it is too detailed.

The bridge should feel obvious, not persuasive.
