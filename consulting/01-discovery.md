# Discovery Phase

## Purpose

The discovery phase is designed to understand how the cloud environment evolved, how intentional the current foundation is, and how safe it is to change or reproduce.

This phase focuses on clarity, not solutions.

---

## Core Areas of Discovery

### Environment Evolution

- How did the cloud environment initially start?
- What were the original goals or constraints?
- How did the environment grow over time?
- Which decisions were intentional, and which were reactive?

---

### Standardization

- What aspects of the environment are standardized today?
- How consistent are environments across teams or subscriptions?
- Where are conventions relied on instead of documented standards?
- How are naming, tagging, and access patterns enforced?

---

### Reproducibility and Change Safety

- Could the current environment be recreated from scratch?
- Which parts would be risky or unclear to reproduce?
- How confident is the team when making foundational changes?
- What happens when a new environment is needed?

---

## Signals to Listen For

During discovery, particular attention is paid to phrases such as:

- "It grew organically"
- "It mostly works"
- "It depends"
- "We avoid touching that part"
- "Only a few people understand this"

These are not problems by themselves, but indicators of potential risk.

---

## Common Risk Indicators

- Heavy reliance on manual or undocumented steps
- Environment-specific behavior or exceptions
- Inconsistent governance across environments
- Fear of making changes to core components

---

## What Discovery Is Not

- A technical deep dive
- A tool evaluation
- An architecture review
- A solution proposal

The goal of discovery is to establish a shared understanding of the current state and its risks.
