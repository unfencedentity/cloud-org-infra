# Package 1 Pitch: Core Cloud Foundation

## Purpose

This pitch introduces the core engagement in a concise, outcome-focused way.

It is designed to explain *what changes* for the client, not *how the work is done*.

---

## Pitch Constraints

The pitch should be:

- 60–90 seconds
- outcome-focused
- tool-agnostic
- free of technical deep dives

If it requires explanation, it is too detailed.

---

## Pitch Structure

### Problem Reminder

Briefly restate the framed issue:

- lack of predictability
- difficulty changing safely
- increasing risk as the environment grows

---

### Outcome Statement

Describe what the client gets *after* the work is done:

- a standardized and reproducible cloud foundation
- clear ownership and operating model
- built-in governance, security, and observability
- an environment that is safe to automate and scale

---

### Scope Boundaries

Clarify what this package focuses on:

- foundation and baseline
- not application refactoring
- not feature development
- not ongoing operations

---

## Example Pitch

"At this stage, what I typically help teams with is establishing a predictable and standardized cloud foundation.

The goal is to make the environment safe to change, easy to reproduce, and ready for automation and growth.

Once that foundation is in place, everything built on top of it becomes simpler and less risky."

---

## What This Pitch Avoids

- naming tools or technologies
- explaining implementation details
- comparing approaches
- discussing pricing or timelines

---

## Notes

If the pitch turns into a discussion, return to outcomes.

Details belong in the assessment, not in the pitch.
