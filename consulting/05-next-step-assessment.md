# Next Step: Initial Technical Assessment

## Purpose

The initial assessment is a small, focused engagement designed to create clarity before any larger commitment.

Its goal is to replace assumptions with facts.

---

## Why an Assessment First

At this stage, jumping directly into implementation introduces unnecessary risk.

The assessment allows both sides to:

- validate the problem framing
- understand the true scope
- align on priorities
- reduce uncertainty before committing further

---

## Assessment Characteristics

The assessment is intentionally:

- time-boxed
- low-commitment
- paid
- focused on clarity, not delivery

It is not an audit or a full redesign.

---

## What the Assessment Produces

Typical outputs include:

- a clear view of the current foundation state
- identified gaps in standardization and governance
- risks related to reproducibility and change
- a concrete recommendation for next steps

---

## How It Is Positioned

The assessment is presented as a decision-support step:

- it informs whether to proceed
- it defines what implementation would actually involve
- it helps avoid over- or under-scoping

---

## What This Step Avoids

- committing to implementation prematurely
- promising specific outcomes
- detailed architecture design
- long-term contracts

---

## Notes

If the assessment feels like selling, it is positioned incorrectly.

Its value is clarity, not momentum.
